flexure flexure

Enter the measured gravitational flexure of the telescope.

Arguments:
Flexure flexure
The gravitational flexure of the mount (degrees per cosine of elevation).

Example:
The following example tells the control system that to point at an elevation of el degrees it must actually point the telescope at an elevation of: el' = el - 0:0:0.5 * cos(el) degrees. 
  flexure 0:0:0.5
Context:
As the elevation of the telescope changes, the load distribution on the mount changes. This can lead to an elevation dependent elevation offset. The design of the BICEP should make this constant insignificant, but it remains to be seen if this is the case.
