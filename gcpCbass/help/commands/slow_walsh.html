slow_walsh on|off

Turn slow walshing on or off.

Arguments:
WalshState state
The desired state of the slow walshing: on or off.

Context:
In addition to the fast hardware Walsh switching, this command allows removal of constant correlator offsets by performing a second level of slow walshing of the receiver LO phases. On each interrupt, the phases of each receiver are modulated by 0 or 180°, according to a set of period-16 Walsh functions, where my convention is -1 --> 180°, +1 --> 0°. Each visibility is demodulated in software with the product of the two receiver Walsh functions prior to software integration. The slow walshing phase shifts are combined with any use-requested phase shifts effected with the quad and only the user-requested values appear in the rx.lo_quad registers.

Since a change of phase of 180° in one LO results in a sign change of the visibility, any constant offset from the correlator will cancel over a complete period of the walsh functions, since the product of any two walsh functions integrates to zero.

Note that when slow walshing is turned off, the change will not take effect until the completion of the current walshing period (16 register snapshots). The bitmask of receiver walsh states is recorded in the frame.walshstate register.
