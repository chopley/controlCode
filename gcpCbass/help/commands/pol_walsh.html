pol_walsh receivers, step, wstep

Set one or more receivers to a particular step in their polarization walsh sequences.

Arguments:
Receivers ant
The set of receivers whose polarization states are to be modified.
PhaseStep step
The step size, from:
  • full - Full steps of the stepper motor.
  • half - Half steps of the stepper motor.
WalshStep wstep
The walsh function step (0-15).

Example:
The following example sets the polarization states of receivers rx0 and rx9 to the configuration given by the first step of their walsh sequences: 
 pol_walsh rx0+rx9, half, 0

Context:
When observing with polarized receivers, we require observations at LL, RR, LR and RL to recover the full set of Stokes parameters. This is accomplished efficiently by stepping the polarization state of each receiver according to one of sixteen orthogonal Walsh functions (1 corresponding to left circular, and -1 to right circular polarization). Since Walsh functions integrate to 0 (except for the lowest order one), over a complete period of the Walsh functions, each receiver will spend equal time in either polarization state. Since the product of two Walsh functions is another Walsh function, each baseline will furthermore spend equal times in the parallel (LL and RR) and crossed (LR and RL) polarization states, and, as it turns out, equal time in each of the LL, RR, LR and RL states.

This function lets you explicitly set the polarization states of a set of receivers to one step in the Walsh sequence.

The Walsh functions used are the same ones used in the slow walshing (see slow_walsh), and are of period 16, generated from the Hadamard matrices (as described, for example, in TMS 7.5). I use the highest-order 13 of these.
